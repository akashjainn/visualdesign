Music Poster: Solange "Neon Bloom" Tour

Typographic hierarchy and genre-specific design for a music tour poster

Project Brief

This project challenged me to create a digital poster for a music tour that combines visual hierarchy with typographic systems to communicate complex information—30+ concert dates and locations—in an accessible and design-forward way. The poster needed to balance aesthetic appeal with functional clarity, allowing viewers to quickly find what matters to them.

Design Thinking

Conveying Mood & Genre

Bold neon greens, vibrant oranges, and striking blacks create an energetic, contemporary aesthetic that reflects Solange's experimental R&B sound and modern sensibility. The bright pops of color against the darker background convey both elegance and youthful energy.

Information Hierarchy & Navigation

The poster uses a clear typographic system: the tour name "Solange" is the dominant visual element using a thick, distinctive serif treatment. Tour dates are organized into two columns (left/right hemispheres) by month, allowing viewers to quickly scan for their location. The layout prioritizes scanability—viewers can find their city without reading every date, while the overall design remains visually striking.

Key Typographic Choices

The hand-drawn, slightly irregular serif letterforms for the main title convey personality and movement. Contrasting this, the tour dates use clean, geometric sans-serif typography for maximum legibility. Different text weights and positioning create rhythm and visual interest while maintaining clarity. The yellow subtitle ("The Neon Bloom Tour 2024") breaks up the composition and adds a secondary focal point.

Composition Strategy

The center is anchored by the artist's portrait, with the tour name flowing across the top in a bold arc. Dates flow around the figure in organized columns, respecting the figure as the focal point while using the negative space of the corners for the strongest design elements (the large orange curved shapes). This creates both visual balance and functional hierarchy.

Feedback Incorporated

After initial feedback, I refined the contrast between the title and body text—increasing the size difference to improve scanability. The color palette was adjusted to ensure dates were legible against their background, and spacing between tour locations was increased for clarity at smaller viewing sizes.
